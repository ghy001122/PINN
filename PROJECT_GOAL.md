# Project Goal

## Goal Identity

- Goal ID: `Q2_SCI_DELIVERY_MODE`
- Delivery target: continuously advance `ghy001122/PINN` until it contains an evidence-sufficient, logically closed, reproducible, academically ethical Q2 SCI manuscript draft, submission package, supplementary material, and reviewer-defense matrix.
- Acceptance boundary: this is a project-delivery target, not a guarantee of journal acceptance.

## North-Star Scientific Claim

The current `GeoPhase-OQ-PINN` candidate claim is deliberately falsifiable: a geometry-aware complete phase-transition PINN can reproduce the field, port, event, interface, and energy behavior of a source-traceable 2.5D VO2 neuristor reference across held-out within-family geometries when its spectral capacity is localized to the transition manifold and training follows a phase-sharpness/electrothermal-feedback homotopy. For inverse use, the PINN must preserve the independent solver's sensitivity subspace and recover only branch/protocol-conditioned observation-quotient coordinates, with refusal outside the identifiable region. This claim is not established until the E0 reference solver, forward PINN, matched-budget ablations, geometry holdouts, sensitivity fidelity, fresh-case inverse, and refusal gates all pass.

The project-generated evidence remains literature-guided synthetic numerical digital-twin evidence. Qiu-inspired VO2 geometry is the primary benchmark; Chen-inspired SnSe/NbO2 is a material-specific cross-model numerical validation only. The supported sparse-port boundary and calibration-gated constrained `gamma_sub` result remain historical evidence and low-dimensional baselines, not the final physical structure or positive neural mainline.

## Mandatory Research Filter

Every task must directly support at least one of:

- the core claim;
- a main figure or table;
- a method equation;
- a baseline or ablation;
- generalization, robustness, or uncertainty evidence;
- reviewer defense;
- a publishable negative result;
- reproducibility or the submission package.

Do not execute tasks whose manuscript value cannot be stated. Select one active bottleneck per round using:

\[
\frac{\text{manuscript value}\times\text{probability of useful evidence}\times\text{reviewer-defense value}}
{\text{time}\times\text{compute}\times\text{risk}}.
\]

## Evidence Lifecycle

Every actual experiment follows:

```text
config -> implementation -> test -> JSON/CSV -> figure/table -> report -> claim matrix -> manuscript sentence
```

Smoke, finite output, loss decrease, a local Jacobian, a response surface, mini-STL, or Fourier integration is not an automatic positive claim.

Every round must close with:

- actual completed work;
- change in distance to the delivery goal;
- claim upgrades or downgrades;
- new blockers;
- the next single highest-priority task;
- a disposition: continue, stop, downgrade, or move to manuscript assembly.

Update the compact status chain and relevant registries without duplicating the same prose.

The detailed reusable workflow, run classes, gate card, and failure routing live in `docs/research_strategy/sci_delivery_pipeline.md`.

## Must-Have Definition Of Done

- Frozen GT and all mainline evidence reproduce from documented commands.
- The independent 2.5D reference passes manufactured, current-conservation, energy-ledger, mesh, time-step, reduction, and single/dual-device limit gates.
- A complete phase-transition PINN produces a positive field/port/event/ledger result on the 2.5D benchmark; if only a hybrid sparse-anchor variant passes, it is labeled `hybrid PINN`.
- Transition-localized architecture and homotopy claims pass matched-budget, multi-seed, stiffness-regime, and single-module ablations; otherwise they are downgraded or removed.
- Within-family geometry holdouts pass without full-network test refitting.
- Any observation-quotient inverse is preceded by solver sensitivity convergence and PINN sensitivity fidelity, is tested on fresh nonlinear/noisy cases, and includes refusal.
- The public-data source-reproduction/identifiability bridge remains dispositioned with provenance and explicit source-paper/refit/cross-voltage/independent-validation semantics.
- Qiu and Chen sources remain literature anchors; project outputs are not called experimental data or exact device reproductions.
- Main figures, main tables, Methods equations, claim matrix, and manuscript sentences map one-to-one.
- A complete manuscript draft and supplementary material exist.
- Code/data provenance and explicit reproduction commands exist.
- A reviewer-defense matrix covers likely objections and negative evidence.
- Every positive manuscript claim is `supported` or `qualified_supported`.
- No unresolved contradiction exists between code, outputs, reports, registries, and manuscript wording.

## Upgrade Gates

- G0 requires all configured manufactured, current/energy ledger, mesh, time-step, K-state reduction, and limit gates to pass. Passing conservation without convergence is insufficient.
- G1 requires field, port, phase-event, interface, and ledger gates to pass jointly on fresh solver cases. Port fit alone is insufficient.
- G2 requires matched budgets and fixed seeds; sharp-regime gains may not conceal smooth-region regression or failed seeds.
- G3 requires a locked geometry/protocol split and no full-network refit on holdouts.
- G4 requires solver Jacobian convergence, PINN trajectory and sensitivity/subspace fidelity, nonlinear fresh-case quotient recovery, and refusal. Trajectory agreement alone cannot authorize inverse claims.
- G5 requires correct trend direction and quantitative gates under a material-specific NbO2 kernel; it cannot claim VO2-to-NbO2 zero-shot transfer.
- Historical D0/N0/P0--P4 gates remain unchanged and cannot be retroactively upgraded by the new benchmark.

## Stretch Goals

- Canonical Seiler-style multi-head STL.

- Full 3D or phase-field multiphysics.

Stretch failure cannot block paper delivery; it only limits claim level.

## Stop, Lock, Or Redirect Rules

- Gate passed: lock evidence and stop unproductive tuning.
- Budget exhausted without passing: mark `failed_but_informative` and write the boundary result.
- Physical equation, source, coordinate, or observation-model error found: stop extensions and repair the foundation.
- Non-identifiability found: reduce the parameter space, add observations, or downgrade the claim.
- Required real experiments are unavailable: use a provenance-backed public-data anchor or state the limitation.
- Never obtain success by changing GT, altering true labels, deleting failed seeds, hiding failed blocks, or changing thresholds after results are known.
- If G0 fails, stop all GeoPhase training and repair or reject the physical reduction.
- If data-free PINN fails but the preregistered hybrid PINN passes, use the hybrid manuscript route and report the data-free failure.
- If quotient subspaces do not show stable rank/rotation or the PINN sensitivity gate fails, remove the strong OQ claim and retain only a fixed low-rank inverse or identifiability boundary.
