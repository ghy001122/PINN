# Abstract V1 Draft

Sparse electrical measurements of phase-change and memristive oxides constrain terminal conductance but do not necessarily identify the underlying thermal, defect, and switching-state fields. We construct a reproducible one-dimensional synthetic numerical digital-twin benchmark for coupled electro-thermal-defect dynamics and use it to audit inverse identifiability from sparse port observations. Direct hidden-field PINN inversion is shown to be underdetermined in this benchmark, motivating an identifiability-guided reduction of the inverse target. Under fixed or tightly bounded literature/engineering priors, the effective thermal dissipation parameter `gamma_sub` becomes a conditionally recoverable scalar target. Confounding, profile-likelihood, prior-width, off-grid, and calibrated-protocol audits identify `T_sw` as the dominant nuisance parameter and show that calibration-before-inversion is required for stable recovery. The study provides a transparent synthetic benchmark and claim-bounded workflow for reduced thermal-parameter inversion, while explicitly avoiding claims of experimental validation or full-field recovery.
