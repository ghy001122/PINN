# Abstract Final Candidate

Sparse electrical-port measurements are attractive for inverse diagnosis of oxide memristive and phase-change devices, but terminal current and conductance do not uniquely identify the hidden thermal, defect, and switching-state fields. We present a synthetic numerical digital-twin benchmark for a reduced electro-thermal-defect memristive stack and use it to audit sparse-port inverse identifiability. The analysis shows that full hidden-field recovery is ill-posed under port-only observations, motivating an identifiability-guided target-space reduction. Under literature-guided and tightly bounded priors, we formulate a calibration-gated constrained inversion of the effective substrate heat-dissipation parameter `gamma_sub`. Confounding, prior-width, off-grid, protocol, and ODE-backed spot-check audits show that `T_sw` calibration dominates recoverability, while protocol design provides secondary robustness. Supplementary stiffness-continuation, phase-field alignment, and quasi-2D preflight results position the model relative to phase-transition PINN literature without claiming experimental validation or solved two-dimensional inverse diagnosis. The work provides a reproducible sparse-port inverse-identification workflow for synthetic digital-twin benchmarking and defines explicit claim boundaries for future experimental extension.
