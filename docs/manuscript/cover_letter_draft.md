# Cover Letter Draft

Dear Editor,

We submit a method-oriented computational manuscript on sparse-port inverse identification in a synthetic numerical digital-twin benchmark for oxide memristive and phase-change device dynamics. The central contribution is an identifiability-guided reduction from ill-posed hidden-field recovery to a calibration-gated constrained inversion of the effective substrate heat-dissipation parameter `gamma_sub`.

The manuscript is intentionally bounded. It does not report measured device data, does not claim full hidden-field recovery from terminal electrical measurements, and does not claim solved two-dimensional inverse diagnosis. Instead, it provides a reproducible benchmark, confounding audits, calibration tolerance analysis, ODE-backed spot checks, and supplementary stiffness/phase-field/quasi-2D evidence for reviewer defense.

We believe the work is suitable for a computational modeling or applied physics methods journal interested in physics-informed inverse problems, digital-twin benchmarking, and memristive/phase-transition device modeling.

Sincerely,

The authors
