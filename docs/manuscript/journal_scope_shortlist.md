## Provisional rank 1: International Journal of Numerical Modelling: Electronic Networks, Devices and Fields

- Official scope: <https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/10991204/homepage/productinformation.html>
- Official author guidance: <https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/10991204/homepage/forauthors.html>
- Scope match: numerical modeling of electronic networks, devices, and fields;
  coupled and moving-boundary problems are explicitly relevant.
- Submission notes: free-format initial submission; editable Word or LaTeX;
  abstract up to 250 words; up to six keywords; data/funding/conflict
  statements and ORCID requirements must be checked at submission.
- Primary risk: the paper must emphasize the numerical inverse/identifiability
  contribution, not claim fabricated-device performance.

## Provisional rank 2: Journal of Computational Electronics

- Official scope: <https://link.springer.com/journal/10825/aims-and-scope>
- Official submission guidelines: <https://link.springer.com/journal/10825/submission-guidelines>
- Scope match: computational methods, physical models, and simulation of
  electronic materials and devices.
- Submission notes: source files required; LaTeX is recommended; abstract
  150--250 words; four to six keywords; data, funding, competing-interest, and
  AI-use declarations required.
- Primary risk: the journal expects a clearly novel physical model,
  computational strategy, or numerical algorithm. A reduced synthetic inverse
  without experiment may face a higher novelty/validation burden.

Recent audience/novelty comparators:

1. *Rapid time-domain simulation of fractional capacitors with SPICE*, DOI `10.1007/s10825-024-02160-x`.
2. *Efficient computational model for single-molecule optoelectronic devices*, DOI `10.1007/s10825-025-02287-5`.
3. *Efficient quantum transport simulations using multi-band discontinuous Galerkin methods*, DOI `10.1007/s10825-025-02398-z`.
4. *Hybrid single-band carrier transport model*, DOI `10.1007/s10825-025-02333-2`.
5. *Integral equation method for the one-dimensional steady Poisson--Nernst--Planck system*, DOI `10.1007/s10825-023-02092-y`.

## Provisional rank 3: Mathematics and Computers in Simulation

- Official scope: <https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/mathematics-and-computers-in-simulation>
- Official guide for authors: <https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/mathematics-and-computers-in-simulation/publish/guide-for-authors>
- Scope match: modeling, simulation, scientific computing, numerical analysis,
  and algorithms.
- Submission notes: editable equations and SI units; up to six keywords; data,
  author-role, funding, conflict, and AI-use declarations required.
- Primary risk: the paper may be judged to lack a sufficiently general or new
  numerical method because the strongest contribution is an
  application-specific identifiability/calibration workflow.

## Decision boundary

The first journal has the clearest topical fit. The second imposes the greatest
device-novelty/experimental-validation risk. The third imposes the greatest
general numerical-method risk. Final selection requires author judgment,
current quartile verification, article-type confirmation, and a check of the
latest official instructions; this file does not authorize submission.
