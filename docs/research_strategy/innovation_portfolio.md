## Active idea 1: QoI-guided Event-Conditioned complete PINN

- **Nearest prior:** PirateNets (`arXiv:2402.00326`), DB-PINN (`arXiv:2505.11117`), Neural Event ODE (ICLR 2021), XPINN (`arXiv:2109.09444`), DeepONet (DOI `10.1038/s42256-021-00302-5`), and goal-oriented DWR sampling (`arXiv:2604.01835`).
- **Substantive delta:** retain the complete multidomain state/field outputs and physical event ledger, but allocate residual effort to an independently solver-verified identifiable QoI and explicitly abstain when sensitivity/profile gates fail.
- **Fatal flaw:** the present full-PINN forward path fails residual, field, interface-flux, and global-ledger gates; QoI weighting could merely hide those failures.
- **MVE:** after manuscript lock and new authorization, compare one global complete PINN and one event-conditioned/QoI allocation arm on the frozen exact triangle and LTP/LTD protocols, one seed first, with unchanged field/residual/interface/ledger gates and no port labels.
- **Go/stop gate:** expand only if the pilot improves the locked QoI error by at least `20%` while every solver-field, residual, interface, and conservation gate is no worse and finite-difference event sensitivity agrees within `5%`. Stop on any ledger regression or sensitivity failure.
- **Compute cap:** initial MVE `<=2` solver references, `<=3 GPU-h`, `<=6 h`; no seed expansion without the one-seed gate.
- **Allowed claim:** implementation-specific evidence that QoI/event conditioning improves a complete PINN without sacrificing solver/conservation fidelity. **Forbidden:** a new event network, DWR, XPINN, DeepONet, optimizer, trained full-PINN success before gates, or world-first claim.

## Active idea 2: residual-triggered enthalpy/latent-heat upgrade (H1)

- **Nearest prior:** Patra et al., Computer Physics Communications 2025, DOI `10.1016/j.cpc.2025.109854`.
- **Substantive delta:** add enthalpy only after a provenance-backed thermal residual survives H0 parameter uncertainty, then test a conservative independent solver before any PINN loss change.
- **Fatal flaw:** no current external thermal trace diagnoses missing latent heat; adding it now would be unsupported model freedom.
- **MVE:** one fixed heating/cooling thermal batch; H0 versus one literature-fixed H1 solver, no PINN training.
- **Go/stop gate:** held-out thermal NRMSE improvement `>=20%`, unchanged energy-ledger tolerance, effect stable to source-parameter uncertainty; otherwise stop.
- **Compute cap:** `<=30` solver forwards, `0 GPU-h`, `<=2 h`.
- **Allowed claim:** `qualified_supported` need for an enthalpy term in the declared trace/model pair. **Forbidden:** universal VO2 latent heat or experimental PINN validation.

## Active idea 3: branch/minor-loop history-state discrepancy gate (H2)

- **Nearest prior:** Nag et al., Journal of Applied Physics 2012, DOI `10.1063/1.4764040`, and Zhang et al., Nature Communications 2024, DOI `10.1038/s41467-024-51254-4`.

- **Compute cap:** `<=40` solver forwards, `0 GPU-h`, `<=3 h`.
- **Allowed claim:** branch-history evidence for the declared source/model. **Forbidden:** generic hysteresis discovery or unique microscopic-state recovery.
